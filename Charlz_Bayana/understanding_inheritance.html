Understanding Inheritance

How traits are inherited?

Inheritance is the process by which genetic information is passed on from parents to their offspring. The principles of inheritance were first outlined by Gregor Mendel through his work on pea plants.

Mendellian Genetics:

Law of Segregation: Each organism contains two alleles for each trait, which segregate during gamete formation.

Law of Independent Assortment: Alleles for different traits are distributed to sex cells (& offspring) independently of one another.

Types of Inheritance:

Dominant and Recessive Traits: Dominant traits mask the presence of recessive ones.

Co-dominance and Incomplete Dominance: Situations where neither allele is dominant.

Polygenic Inheritance: Traits controlled by two or more genes.
